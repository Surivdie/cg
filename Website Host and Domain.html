ChatGPT said:

Great question! Let's break it down simply:

1. What is a domain?

  • A domain is your website's address on the internet — like www.yourname.com.

  • It’s what people type in the browser to find your site.

  • You usually pay a yearly fee to register and keep ownership of your domain.

2. What is hosting?

  • A host is the server where all your website’s files, images, and content are stored.

  • Think of it like renting space on the internet so people can actually visit your site.

  • Hosting providers make sure your site is always available (online) and loads quickly.

3. Why you need both:

  • The domain is like the street address.

  • The hosting is like the house at that address.

  • Without hosting, your domain points to nothing. Without a domain, no one knows where to find your hosted site.

4. How platforms like Squarespace help:

  • Squarespace (and similar platforms like Wix or Webflow) bundle both services.

  • You can buy a domain through them and they host your site — all in one place.

  • They also give you tools to design the website easily, so you don’t have to code anything.
